course
description

Graphic design in contemporary society is the most effective form of communication in our everyday life. The class focuses on the intersection between visual communication and social issues using hands-on and computational tools. The class through the lens of elements of design, such as shape, color, typography, geometry to describe social issues or express our concern in today’s society that we encounter every day. The class through the lens of color, layout, typography, geometry as the typical topics in graphic design study, to describe the social issues that we encounter every day, such as design for activists, boundary, race, globalization in the formats of sign, poster, postcard, and zine. Topics will be around activists, boundaries, social distance, globalization, etc, in making of sign, poster, postcard, and zine. The class will be a mix of lecture, discussion, and hands-on experience. Participants will use design as a medium to express their thinking on social issues and produce both digital visual work and physical sculptural object as zine or artist’s book. In each session, there will be space for questions, debates, conversations, workshops, show and tell. There will be weekly assignments all leading up to a final online showcase.

course outline

Week 1: A moment to get to know each other.

This session is about getting to know each other. There will be introductions of who we all are but also discussions regarding expectations and what you hope to gain from the class. Additionally, the instructor will lead a discussion about the topic of social issues, introducing the concept of this class and giving a brief lecture about graphic design and design languages. Finally, we will introduce some of the tools that we'll be using in class.

Week 2: “Boundary” Workshop

This week, we will look at geometric shapes and explore the symbolic meaning of those basic shapes corresponding to social distance, personal boundaries, comfort zone, intimate relationships, culture differences, etc. After a brief lecture, we'll get started with a hands-on programming workshop allowing us to create and play with these shapes in p5.js/Processing. Students will create works using only these basic shapes to express their thinking of social distance, boundary or related topics. This in-class exercise will become the assignment for the week.

Week 3: “Posters Occupy the Street”

Poster design is one of the most common projects a designer can take on. What began as one of the earliest forms of advertisement, developed as a medium for visual communication in the early 19th century. From the first poster in history to propaganda posters occupying the street, they allow us to make big bold public statements. This class focuses on using code to put posters in the context of protest, leading students to creating powerful messages using typography, while thinking like an activist.

Week 4: “Making a Zine”

Making a Zine is the easiest way to express and distribute your ideas in a relatively underground way. This week, the instructor will give a brief lecture about the history of Artist’s Books and zine culture, and conduct a hands-on workshop about making zines around your most concerning social issue of the moment. Students will use different approaches such as collage, drawing, digital print, cut-off, paint, etc. The assignment begins in class and should be finished before next week’s class’s Show & Tell.

Week 5: Show and Tell, Online Exhibition

In this final session, we’ll come together to see what everyone has made as students present their zine and other works created during the class. The instructor and guest critics will offer constructive feedback throughout the session. The class will end here, but everyone is encouraged to continue with their on-going “Social Design” projects.

who is this
class for?

This class is for graphic designers, illustrators, activists and anyone interested in the intersection of visual communications, social design, and learning to code.

meet the
instructors

Liu Chang

Liu Chang

Artist, Educator

Liu Chang is an artist and educator, currently based in Shanghai and New York. Chang’s work explores the connection between nature and the artificial world by juxtaposing them. Her interactive work always discusses the existence of time. Chang’s art practice involves interactive installation, computational portraiture, novelty material in screen-print, artist book, etc. Her recent research, from an interdisciplinary perspective, focuses on generative art, museum technology and the intersection of algorithmic art and graphic design. She also teaches related courses in IMA, NYU Shanghai.

Chang’s work has been exhibited internationally, at places such as FOU Gallery (New York), Currents New Media Arts Festival (Santa Fe), Carroll/Fletcher Gallery (London), Power Station of Art (Shanghai), The National Museum of China (Beijing), etc. Her work has been featured in many worldwide magazines and art review journals, such as 《YISHU, 《Art Collection, 《Art World,《ARTFORUM.

She holds an M.P.S degree from the Interactive Telecommunications Program (ITP) in the Tisch School of Arts, New York University. She is a Clinical Assistant Professor of Arts in Interactive Media Arts, NYU Shanghai.
